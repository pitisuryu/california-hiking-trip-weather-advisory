# California Hiking Trip Weather Advisory

## General Climate Information
The climate of California varies greatly by region due to the state's diverse geography, from coastal areas to deserts and mountains. Coastal regions, including much of the San Francisco Bay Area and Southern California, have a Mediterranean climate characterized by mild, wet winters and dry summers. Inland valleys experience hot, dry summers and cooler, wetter winters. The Sierra Nevada mountains have a continental climate with heavy snowfall in winter and warm summers. Deserts in the southeast, like the Mojave and Colorado Deserts, are arid with extreme heat. California's climate is influenced by the Pacific Ocean, the rain shadow of coastal mountains, and periodic events like El Niño and La Niña. Precipitation is highest in the north and mountains, lowest in the south and deserts. Recent years have seen increased wildfires, droughts, and heatwaves due to climate change.
